## Where the win comes from

1. **Lower compute when nothing to detect.** Seeds 2, 4, 7 (zero detections in episode): trained spends 5–11 compute units, heuristic spends 48–95. Heuristic's `EMERGENCY_BURST` (3 missed detections → all-on @30 fps) burns compute when there's genuinely nothing to find. Trained learned to ride out the dry spells at the rail floor.
2. **Higher detection on busy scenes.** Seeds 3, 5, 9: trained detection reward is 1.5–4.4× heuristic. Trained keeps thermal active in dim conditions where heuristic only triggers night thermal under R1 (lux<10).
3. **Roughly tied at night-with-rails.** Seed 1 (lux=4.7, DESCEND_CONFIRM): both forced into all-on by R1+R3, reward delta is 0.69 (essentially identical).

## Known eval-display caveat

The `thermal=N/120` and `th_day=N/120` counts show how many ticks thermal was *on*, not at what FPS level. A policy holding thermal=5 (rail floor) for 120 ticks shows the same `120/120` count as one running thermal=30 (burst) for 120 ticks. The `compute=` column captures the real difference (e.g. seed 2 trained=11.20 vs heuristic=94.20 with both showing `thermal=120/120`). Worth fixing the display to show mean fps, but not blocking 5C.
